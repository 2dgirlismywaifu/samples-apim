In rail transport, a train is a series of connected vehicles that run along a railway track and transport people or
freight. The word train comes from the Old French trahiner, derived from the Latin trahere meaning "to pull, to draw".
Trains are typically pulled or pushed by locomotives (often known simply as "engines"), though some are self-propelled,
such as multiple units. Passengers and cargo are carried in railroad cars, also known as wagons. Trains are designed to
a certain gauge, or distance between rails. Most trains operate on steel tracks with steel wheels, the low friction of
which makes them more efficient than other forms of transport.

A railway platform is an area alongside a railway track providing convenient access to trains. Almost all stations have
some form of platform, with larger stations having multiple platforms.

A train shed is a building adjacent to a station building where the tracks and platforms of a railway station are
covered by a roof. It is also known as an overall roof. Its primary purpose is to store and protect from the elements train cars not in use
